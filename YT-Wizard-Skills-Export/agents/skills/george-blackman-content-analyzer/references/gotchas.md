# Gotchas

- Do not praise a script just because it sounds polished.
- Do not judge only the visible weak line. Look earlier for the structural cause.
- Do not confuse specificity with length. A long script can still be vague.
- Do not fix hooks by adding more information. First decide what is critical.
- Do not delete useful material automatically. Redistribute it when possible.
- Do not protect the original voice if it is making the script less credible or less clear.
- Do not nitpick polish when the setup, tension, or payoff chain is broken.
- Do not let the analyzer create fake work. If it is good enough, say `good enough, ship`.
