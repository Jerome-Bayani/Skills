# Workflow

## Mode Selection

- `review_and_fix`
  Use for your own title, hook, outline, or full script when the goal is to diagnose and repair.

- `study_and_extract`
  Use for outside scripts or transcripts when the goal is to understand why they work and how to adapt the strength.

- `review_hook_or_outline`
  Use for early-stage material when a full script does not exist yet.

## George-First Diagnosis Sequence

1. Identify the promised transformation.
2. Check audience and framing clarity.
3. Check hook through target -> transformation -> stakes.
4. Check whether the structure follows setup -> tension -> payoff.
5. Audit setup-stakes strength with `why should I care?`
6. Check whether beneficial information is bloating the hook.
7. Check whether useful material should be redistributed later.
8. Check trust chain segment by segment.
9. Check where confusion likely begins, not just where it becomes visible.
10. Decide whether to patch or rebuild.

## Severity Logic

- `critical`
  Structural or trust-breaking problems that make the asset weak even if the wording sounds polished.

- `important`
  Problems that reduce clarity, authority, or retention but do not destroy the whole asset.

- `polish`
  Wording or rhythm improvements that matter only after the structure is solid.

## Repair Philosophy

- Move material before deleting it when it still has value.
- Tighten hooks by stripping beneficial information first.
- Strengthen setups before blaming the payoff.
- Rebuild the whole thing only when the skeleton is weak.
- Preserve the user's voice when it does not hurt performance.
- Prefer one strongest recommendation over too many options.
