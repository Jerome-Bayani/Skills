# Gotchas

## 1. Hook-First Writing Can Create Fake Clarity

If you write the hook before the body is real, the script often gets bent around a promise that does not match the eventual structure.

## 2. A Good Script Is Not Just A Good Outline

Do not stop at "the structure seems fine."

Production-ready means the spoken script is actually recordable.

## 3. Too Many Options Early Slows Everything Down

Recommend one main direction first.

Only add a couple of alternates where it materially helps, such as title and hook.

## 4. Research Added Too Late Creates Rewrites

If a script needs facts, add them before the final draft hardens.

## 5. Spoken Scripts Die When They Sound Like Essays

Write for speech.

Favor natural phrasing, sentence rhythm, and clarity over sounding formally "written."

## 6. Curiosity Can Backfire

Do not stack unresolved curiosity gaps.

Resolve one meaningful question before opening another major one.

## 7. The Tension Phase Is Not Filler

If tension does not deepen the need for the payoff, it becomes drift.

## 8. A Direct Generator Still Needs Checkpoints

The fastest workflow is not "skip checkpoints."

It is "pause where wrong choices become expensive."
